# toshu.ceramics Brand Research

Date: 2026-06-01

## Known Source

- Primary public handle: <https://www.instagram.com/toshu.ceramics/>
- Public web search did not surface a separate indexed website, marketplace, press profile, or location page for `toshu.ceramics`.
- Direct Instagram content may require browser/session access, so detailed claims about catalog, pricing, location, artist biography, shipping, and availability still need confirmation.

## Working Hypothesis

`toshu.ceramics` should be treated as a small handmade ceramics studio or artist-led ceramics brand until proven otherwise. The website should therefore lead with the work itself: objects, textures, scale, process, availability, and contact.

## Site Jobs

- Establish trust quickly for visitors arriving from Instagram.
- Show the ceramic objects with enough image quality to understand shape, glaze, texture, and scale.
- Make purchase or inquiry paths obvious without making the site feel like a mass-market store.
- Preserve the studio's tone: quiet, tactile, human, and product-focused.
- Provide a durable home outside social media for drops, archive, care instructions, and commissions.

## Content To Request

- Studio or artist name, location, and preferred language mix.
- Short story: how the studio started, materials, firing process, and values.
- Product categories: cups, bowls, vases, plates, sculptural pieces, custom work, workshops, or other lines.
- Current selling flow: Instagram DM, marketplace, pickup, delivery, shipping, preorders, drops.
- Real product photos and videos with usage rights for the site.
- Pricing rules, sold/archive policy, care instructions, and contact details.

## First Design Direction

- Use real ceramics as the first-viewport signal.
- Prefer editorial product photography, not abstract gradients or generic craft imagery.
- Build a restrained palette from clay, glaze, oxide, paper, and kiln-shadow tones.
- Keep typography calm and legible; let product forms carry the character.
- Make the home page useful immediately: featured pieces, collections, process, contact.

## Open Questions

- Is `toshu.ceramics` a studio, personal artist brand, shop, workshop, or mixed practice?
- Where is the studio based?
- Should the site be Russian-first, English-first, bilingual, or another language?
- Is the commercial goal immediate sales, portfolio credibility, commissions, waitlist, or event/workshop booking?
- Are there existing logo, marks, packaging, labels, or typography references?
